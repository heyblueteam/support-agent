# Escalation Procedures

## When to Escalate

### Technical Escalation
- Issue requires developer/engineering input
- Bug affects multiple customers
- Security vulnerability suspected
- Service outage or performance degradation
- Integration or API issues

### Customer Escalation
- Customer requests manager/supervisor
- Complaint about service quality
- Billing disputes over $X amount
- Threat of cancellation or legal action
- Customer expresses extreme frustration

### Internal Escalation
- Issue unresolved after 3 business days
- Requires cross-team coordination
- Policy exception needed
- Resource allocation required

## Escalation Process

### Step 1: Document Everything
- Full customer history
- Previous interactions
- Steps already taken
- Current issue status
- Customer impact assessment

### Step 2: Internal Handoff
- Use escalation email template
- Include all relevant context
- Set clear expectations
- Copy customer-facing agent

### Step 3: Customer Communication
```
Hi [Customer Name],

I've escalated your issue to our [team/specialist] who can provide more specialized assistance.

[Name] will be reaching out to you within [timeframe] to continue working on your [issue description].

I'll continue to monitor the progress and ensure you receive the help you need.

Best regards,
[Agent Name]
```

### Step 4: Follow-up
- Check status within 24 hours
- Ensure customer receives communication
- Document resolution or next steps

## De-escalation Strategies

### For Frustrated Customers
1. Acknowledge their frustration
2. Apologize for the inconvenience
3. Take ownership of the resolution
4. Provide clear next steps and timeline
5. Offer compensation if appropriate

### For Technical Issues
1. Explain the technical complexity
2. Provide workarounds if available
3. Give realistic timelines
4. Offer regular updates
5. Ensure proper resources are assigned

## Post-Escalation Follow-up

### Customer Follow-up
- Check satisfaction after resolution
- Gather feedback on escalation process
- Document lessons learned
- Update customer notes

### Internal Review
- Analyze root cause
- Update processes if needed
- Train team on new procedures
- Report trends to management
