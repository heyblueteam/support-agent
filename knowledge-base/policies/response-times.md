# Response Time Guidelines

## Standard Response Times

### Priority Levels

**Critical/Urgent Issues**
- Initial response: Within 4 hours
- Examples: Service outages, security issues, payment failures
- Follow-up: Every 6 hours until resolved

**High Priority**
- Initial response: Within 24 hours
- Examples: Functionality not working, data access issues
- Follow-up: Every 48 hours

**Medium Priority**
- Initial response: Within 48 hours
- Examples: Feature questions, minor bugs, general inquiries
- Follow-up: Weekly until resolved

**Low Priority**
- Initial response: Within 72 hours
- Examples: Feature requests, documentation questions
- Follow-up: As needed

## Response Time Considerations

### Factors Affecting Response Time
- Time zones and business hours
- Complexity of the issue
- Need for internal consultation
- Availability of technical resources

### When to Acknowledge Delays
Always acknowledge when response will exceed standard times:

```
"Thank you for your patience. Due to [reason], our response time may be longer than usual. We expect to get back to you by [date/time]."
```

## Escalation Triggers

### Immediate Escalation
- Customer expresses frustration about response time
- Issue has been open for more than 2x standard response time
- Customer threatens to cancel or mentions competitor
- Technical issue requires specialist knowledge

### How to Escalate
1. Tag message with appropriate escalation label
2. Include summary of issue and customer history
3. Note any time-sensitive aspects
4. Copy relevant team members

## Auto-Response Guidelines

### When to Use Auto-Responses
- Initial acknowledgment during off-hours
- For common questions with standard answers
- To set expectations for response time

### What to Include
- Acknowledgment of receipt
- Expected response timeframe
- Emergency contact info if applicable
- Link to self-service resources

## Measuring Performance

### Key Metrics
- First response time
- Resolution time
- Customer satisfaction scores
- Escalation rates

### Reporting
- Weekly response time reports
- Monthly trend analysis
- Quarterly customer satisfaction review
